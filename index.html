Creating Scroll-Based Animations in Full View()

CSS-Tricks

Discover Scroll-Driven CSS Animations: A Better Way to Animate on Scroll
Tired of relying on JavaScript for scroll-triggered animations? There's a new CSS feature that's changing the game. In a recent deep dive, I explored the `animation-timeline` property and its powerful `view()` function. Here's why you should check it out:
What It Is
CSS `view()` creates scroll-driven animations that respond to element visibility instead of time. Think of it as `IntersectionObserver` built directly into CSS—no JavaScript required!
The Carousel Example
The article demonstrates a smooth image carousel where:
- Images are small and blurry at the edges
- They grow and sharpen as they reach the center
- Everything happens purely with CSS as you scroll
How It Works (The Simple Part)
Step 1 Write normal CSS animations with `@keyframes` (nothing new to learn!)
Step 2 Attach them to a `view()` timeline instead of the default timeline
Step 3 Watch animations sync perfectly with scrolling.

Practical XAI
Dec 5, 2025

Beyond The Black Box: Practical XAI For UX Practitioners

Smashing Magazine

Building Trust in AI Through Explainable Design
Why do AI decisions often feel like a frustrating black box? My latest article explores how UX professionals can make AI systems transparent, understandable, and trustworthy through Explainable AI (XAI) design.
Why XAI Matters When AI denies a loan, recommends strange content, or makes unexpected decisions, users lose trust. XAI bridges the gap between algorithmic outputs and human understanding, transforming confusion into clarity and building sustainable trust. Key XAI Methods for Designers The article breaks down practical techniques you can implement:
Feature Importance. Show users what factors mattered most in a decision.
Counterfactuals. Answer "what would I need to change?" to get different results.
Value-Based Explanations. Visualize how each factor pushed decisions positively or negatively.
Practical Design Patterns Learn how to translate XAI concepts into intuitive interfaces:
"Because" Statements Simple plain-language explanations.
Interactive "What-If" Tools Give users agency to explore alternatives.
Visual Push-Pull Displays Show competing factors influencing decisions.
Progressive Disclosure Provide just the right amount of detail when users need it. The UX Researcher's Crucial Role Discover how to use mental model interviews and AI journey mapping to:
Identify: exactly when and where explanations are needed.
Validate: whether explanations actually build understanding and trust.
Uncover: hidden user assumptions about how AI works.
Beyond Trust: Ethical Responsibility XAI isn't just about user experience—it's essential for identifying algorithmic bias and preventing "explanation washing" that obscures unfair outcomes.
